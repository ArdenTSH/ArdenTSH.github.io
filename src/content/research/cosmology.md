This is where my current work lives. At Cambridge I'm taking the MASt in
Astrophysics, working toward a PhD in theoretical cosmology and the physics of
the very early universe.

The thread I'm pulling on is the **cosmological collider**: heavy fields present
during inflation leave faint, oscillatory imprints in the correlations of
primordial fluctuations — a particle spectrometer written across the sky. My
question is whether those signatures survive into the **scalar-induced
gravitational-wave** background: does a fingerprint designed for the microwave
sky also leave one we could one day hear?

## What I'm working on

- Inflationary correlators and the symmetries that constrain their shape.
- Mapping cosmological-collider features onto the induced gravitational-wave spectrum.
